Boom Deployment Mechanism

Research assistant for Cornell Space Structures Lab. Working on a mechanism to deploy an ultra-lightweight boom from a CubeSat without buckling or blossoming.

The Challenge: Blossoming

Deploying lightweight composite booms from small satellites (CubeSats) presents a unique challenge. The stored energy in the coiled boom can cause it to "blossom"—expanding uncontrollably inside the canister—which leads to jamming or structural failure.

The Solution: Membrane Constraint

To solve this, I designed a low-friction membrane that surrounds the boom at every region within the deployment mechanism, except where the boom exits the CubeSat. This membrane holds the coil tight against the springs that are attempting to revert to their original length.

First Prototype: Utilized 4 sets of springs.
Current Prototype: Uses 2 sets of springs with membrane constraint (75% retention success).
