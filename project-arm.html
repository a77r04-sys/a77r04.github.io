Structure of Robotic Manipulator

SolidWorksANSYS FEAMillingMechatronics

In my junior year, I designed the structure of the robotic manipulator for the Cornell Mars Rover Project Team. I redesigned the locations of the motors, moving one of the motors to the elbow of the manipulator, in order to reduce the moment created on the base of the robotic manipulator. I took the lead in assembling, wiring, and testing the arm. My Final Design Review presentation can be found at: FDR presentation link.

Detailed view of joint movement and elbow redesign

5kg Load Testing

This second video demonstrates the successful testing of the arm picking up the 5kg cache. By moving the motor mass closer to the elbow, we validated that the reduced moment allows for stable lifting operations at full extension.
