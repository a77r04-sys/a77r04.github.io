Robotic Manipulator Structure

Lead of the arm sub-team for the Cornell Mars Rover project. The manipulator is a custom 6-DOF robotic arm designed to balance heavy lifting capabilities with fine motor dexterity.

Design Requirements

The arm is engineered to meet the strict requirements of the University Rover Competition (URC). It must be capable of lifting loads up to 5kg (such as rocks or equipment caches) while maintaining the precision required for dexterous tasks like typing on a keyboard, screwing in fasteners, moving a joystick, and flipping switches.

Manufacturing & Integration

My role involves overseeing the full lifecycle of the arm, from initial CAD design to manufacturing and integration. The system utilizes a combination of high-torque motors and custom machined structural components to ensure rigidity and reliability in a simulated Martian environment.
