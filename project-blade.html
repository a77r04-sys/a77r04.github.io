Airfoil Blade Design

Skills MATLAB Autodesk Fusion
Final blade geometry in Fusion 360

For MAE 4272 (Heat Transfer and Fluids Laboratory), we were tasked with designing an airfoil to operate at a fixed rotation rate in wind conditions described by a Weibull distribution — a constrained optimization problem.

I chose to optimize for power at a target rotation rate of 1000 RPM. Using our Weibull distribution, I calculated the optimal design wind speed of 5.05 m/s. The peak is visible in the figure to the right.

Weibull distribution and expected power generation

To optimize power, I maximized the Cl/Cd ratio — designing for a constant angle of attack of about 9.75°. The blade's pitch was twisted along its span to hold that angle of attack, and the chord was tapered to maximize power within constraints. The stress prediction at the highest probable wind speed is shown to the right.

Expected stress on airblade

We tested the airfoils in a wind tunnel, collecting power curves from 3.4 m/s to 7.1 m/s — our upper limit of likely wind speeds. Results showed that the airfoils did peak near 1000 RPM as designed, but generated power was an order of magnitude below prediction. Probability-scaled power curves are below.
