For my class MAE 4272, we were asked to design an airfoil. The goal was to design an airfoil to operate at a fixed rotation rate, while operating in wind conditions told to us according to a Weibull distribution. We chose to optimize our design for power at our chosen rotation rate of 1000 RPM. Given our Weibull Distribution, we calculated which wind speed to design for, which was determined to be 5.05 m/s.

Designed Airblade geometry

To optimize our power, we designed for a constant angle of attack of about 9.75 degrees. We twisted the pitch angle to maintain this angle of attack and tapered our chord length. We obtaining power curves from 3.4 m/s to 7.1 m/s. Our results showed the most power near 1000 RPM; however, power was an order of magnitude less than expected. My work involved developing the code and main design choices.

Technologies Used: MATLAB, Autodesk Fusion
