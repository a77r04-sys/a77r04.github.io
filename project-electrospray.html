Electrospray Testing & Manufacturing

Skills Analysis CAD Soldering LabVIEW

As a research assistant for the Cornell ASTRA Lab, I tested and analyzed novel nanoparticle fluids in electrospray experiments to determine whether the propellants could last 10× longer than current technology.

Tests were performed by generating electric fields at very high voltages, splitting nanoparticles into cations and anions and accelerating the attracted particles through an aperture in stainless steel plates. Thrust and specific impulse were measured via a conductive collector plate and a high-voltage sensing circuit. Testing took place in a vacuum chamber, so precision was critical — I implemented a double-goniometer setup allowing fine rotation of the nanoparticle emitter.
