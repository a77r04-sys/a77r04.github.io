About

This past spring, I graduated summa cum laude from Cornell University with a degree in mechanical engineering. This summer, I am a Structural Mechanics and Concepts Intern at NASA Langley Research Center.

At Cornell, I led the arm sub-team of the Cornell Mars Rover project, building a manipulator for the University Rover Competition. The summer before, as a Deployable Technologies Intern at Physical Sciences Inc., I designed a gripper enabling long-distance carry of rescue litters, tested a low-lubricity fluid pump, and contributed to a lightweight mobile folding bridge.

I also worked with the Cornell ASTRA Lab on novel propellants for electrospray thrusters, and from my sophomore year on, was a research assistant at the Cornell Space Structures Lab designing a boom-deployment mechanism for ultra-lightweight booms.

Coursework included Dynamics, Fluids, Mechanics of Materials, Mechatronics, Heat Transfer, Intermediate Dynamics, and Spacecraft Technology and Systems Architecture.
